Welcome to Distributed Systems

Distributed systems consist of multiple computers that communicate and coordinate their actions by passing messages. They work together as a single system to achieve a common goal.

Client-Server Model

A central server provides resources or services to multiple clients over a network.

Peer-to-Peer

All nodes are equal participants that share resources without centralized coordination.

Microservices

An architectural style where applications are built as independent, scalable services.

Consistency & Consensus

Protocols like Paxos and Raft ensure data consistency across distributed nodes.
